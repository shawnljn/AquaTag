# Fabrication and validation

This folder will document the complete build process from assembled PCB to a tested AquaTag device.

Planned documents:

- Tools, consumables, PPE, and workspace requirements
- PCBA inspection and dry functional test
- Firmware programming and pre-potting test
- Battery, microSD, barometer, LEDs, and charging-contact installation
- Epoxy product, mix ratio, quantity, dispensing steps, cure conditions, and acceptance criteria
- Post-cure electrical and wireless checks
- Leak/pressure-test apparatus, staged procedure, pass/fail criteria, and test log template
- Rinsing, drying, storage, inspection, and retirement guidance

## Safety note

Epoxy potting is an established waterproof-packaging technique; AquaTag adapts it to this wearable form factor. Every independently fabricated unit must be treated as unvalidated until inspected and tested. Follow the resin and battery manufacturers' safety instructions, use suitable PPE and ventilation, and never charge wet or contaminated contacts.

The manuscript reports prototype operation for 30 minutes at each stage up to 20 psi (approximately 14.06 m equivalent hydrostatic depth). This is a controlled prototype result, not a certification or universal depth rating.
